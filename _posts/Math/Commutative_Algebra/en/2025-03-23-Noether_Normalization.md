## Noether Normalization

The goal of this post is to prove the following theorem and examine its consequences.

<div class="proposition" markdown="1">

<ins id="thm1">**Theorem 1 (Noether normalization lemma)**</ins> Let $$A$$ be a finitely generated $$d$$-dimensional $$\mathbb{K}$$-algebra. Suppose we are given natural numbers satisfying the inequality

$$d_1>d_2>\cdots>d_m>0$$

and a descending chain of ideals of $$A$$

$$\mathfrak{a}_1\subset \mathfrak{a}_2\subset\cdots\subset \mathfrak{a}_m$$

with $$\dim \mathfrak{a}_i=d_i$$. Then there exists a suitable subring $$B\cong \mathbb{K}[\x_1,\ldots, \x_d]$$ of $$A$$ such that $$A$$ is finitely generated as a $$B$$-module and

$$\mathfrak{a}_i\cap B=(\x_{d_i+1},\ldots, \x_d)\qquad\text{for $i=1,\ldots, m$}$$

holds.

</div>

This can be proved using the following lemma, whose proof we omit.

<div class="proposition" markdown="1">

<ins id="lem2">**Lemma 2**</ins> Let $$\mathbb{K}$$ be a field and let $$f\in B=\mathbb{K}[\x_1,\ldots, \x_r]$$ be a non-constant polynomial. Then there exist suitable elements $$\x_1',\ldots, \x_{r-1}'\in B$$ such that, letting $$B'$$ be the $$\mathbb{K}$$-subalgebra of $$B$$ generated by $$\x_1',\ldots, \x_{r-1}', f$$, we can arrange that $$B$$ is a finitely generated $$B'$$-module. Moreover, these elements can be chosen as follows.

1. For a sufficiently large integer $$e$$, we can take $$\x_i'=\x_i-\x_r^{e}$$.
2. If $$\mathbb{K}$$ is an infinite field, then for suitable $$a_i\in \mathbb{K}$$ we can take $$\x_i'=\x_i-a_i\x_r$$.

</div>

The proof of [Theorem 1](#thm1) proceeds as follows.

<details class="proof--alone" markdown="1">
<summary>Proof of Theorem 1</summary>

Since $$A$$ is a finitely generated $$\mathbb{K}$$-algebra, we may write $$A=\mathbb{K}[\y_1,\ldots, \y_r]/\mathfrak{a}$$. Given a chain of ideals satisfying the stated conditions, consider the chain formed by their preimages in $$\mathbb{K}[\y_1,\ldots, \y_r]$$

$$\tilde{\mathfrak{a}}_1\subset \tilde{\mathfrak{a}}_2\subset\cdots\subset  \tilde{\mathfrak{a}}_m$$

and insert $$\mathfrak{a}_0=\mathfrak{a}$$ to regard it as a descending chain of ideals in $$\mathbb{K}[\y_1,\ldots, \y_r]$$

$$\mathfrak{a}\subset \tilde{\mathfrak{a}}_1\subset \tilde{\mathfrak{a}}_2\subset\cdots\subset  \tilde{\mathfrak{a}}_m$$

Thus it suffices to prove the claim for the polynomial ring $$A=\mathbb{K}[\y_1,\ldots, \y_r]$$. In this case, [┬¦System of Parameters, ŌüŗCorollary 10](/en/math/commutative_algebra/system_of_parameters#cor10) implies that $$r=d$$.

To construct the elements $$\x_i$$ in the theorem, we first set $$\x_i'=\y_i$$ and then modify them inductively to obtain the desired $$\x_d$$. Assume that elements $$\x_1',\ldots, \x_e', \x_{e+1},\ldots, \x_d$$ satisfying the following two conditions are given:

1. $$A$$ is a finitely generated $$B_e=\mathbb{K}[\x_1',\ldots, \x_e',\x_{e+1},\ldots, \x_d]$$-module.
2. For each $$i$$, we have $$\mathfrak{a}_i\cap B_e\supset(\x_m,\ldots, \x_d)$$, where $$m=\max(d_i+1, e+1)$$.

We show that from these we can produce new elements $$\x_1',\ldots, \x_{e-1}'$$ and $$\x_e$$ so that the above conditions are preserved. Repeating this process, we see that the final $$B=B_{d_m}$$ satisfies the desired conditions provided the inclusion in the second condition is in fact an equality; this is immediate upon comparing the dimensions of the two ideals of $$B$$ on either side.

To complete this induction, fix $$e$$ with $$d\geq e>d_m$$ and suppose that elements $$\x_1',\ldots, \x_e', \x_{e+1},\ldots, \x_d$$ satisfying the two conditions above are given. Let $$i$$ be the smallest index such that $$e>d_i$$. Then

$$\mathfrak{a}_i\cap \mathbb{K}[\x_1',\ldots, \x_e']\neq 0$$

Indeed, if this intersection were $$0$$, then by the second condition we would have

$$\mathfrak{a}_i\cap B_e\supseteq (\x_{e+1},\ldots, \x_d),$$

for the ideal on the left-hand side has dimension $$d_i$$, whereas the ideal on the right-hand side has dimension $$e$$, a contradiction. Now choose $$\x_e$$ to be any nonzero polynomial in this intersection, and then apply [Lemma 2](#lem2) to replace the elements $$\x_1',\ldots, \x_{e-1}'$$ with new ones as well.

</details>

## Consequences

[Theorem 1](#thm1) yields the following result.

<div class="proposition" markdown="1">

<ins id="thm3">**Theorem 3**</ins> Let $$A$$ be an integral domain that is a finitely generated $$\mathbb{K}$$-algebra. Then $$\dim A=\trdeg_\mathbb{K}\Frac(A)$$.

</div>
